The Kuwaiti papers said they hoped the Jiddah meeting was the start of ``a final just settlement'' of the differences between Iraq and Kuwait, in a manner ensuring the ``legitimate rights of each.''
On Tuesday, the Iraqi delegation left Baghdad without any public announcement.
OPEC member states also agreed to a longstanding Iraqi demand to raised target crude oil prices from $18 to $21 a barrel.
Kuwait and the U.A.E agreed to stick to their oil quotas at an OPEC meeting last week.
There were no details on the negotiations, but sources said the meetings will continue Wednesday.
The delegation leaders, Crown Prince and Prime Minister Sheik Saad al-Abdullah al-Sabah of Kuwait, and Izzat Ibrahim, deputy chairman of Iraq's Revolutionary Command Council, met privately for two hours.
