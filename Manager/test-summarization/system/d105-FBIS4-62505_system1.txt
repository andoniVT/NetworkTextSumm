"We are confident that our movement is growing so strong that it cannot be stopped," Shabdolov said.
Apart from the communist party, the founders of the new movement include Tajik trade unions, the Union of Tajik Women, organisations of veterans and ethnic and cultural associations.
The civil war in the Republic claimed the lives of more than 100,000 people, Shabdolov said.
Tajikistan was hit most hard.
He noted that the majority of the population support Tajik communists' call for the restoration of the Soviet Union.
