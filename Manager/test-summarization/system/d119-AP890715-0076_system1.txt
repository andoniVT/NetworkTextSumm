Sarbanes says he is not against political ambassadors as such, noting that many of them compiled distinguished records.
He said the nonpartisan academy has no objections to political appointees as such but does believe political nominees should be qualified to run a complex embassy and represent the United States abroad with ``dignity, respect and ability.''
Gildenhorn, according to Senate sources, is one of several Bush ambassadorial nominees who have been rated ``unqualified'' by the American Academy of Diplomacy, an organization composed of former high ranking diplomats, including all living former secretaries of state.
