In a separate service in Londons Westminster Abbey, attended by Blair, Foreign Secretary Robin Cook and Prince Andrew, the second son of Queen Elizabeth II, hundreds listened as one-by-one the names of the 259 passengers and 11 of Lockerbies own residents were read out as a candle was lit for each.
Earlier in the day, Prince Philip, the queens husband, laid a wreath in Lockerbies cemetery, where the towns Roman Catholic parish priest at the time of the crash spoke to about 200 victims relatives and townspeople about the ticking bomb of justice.
