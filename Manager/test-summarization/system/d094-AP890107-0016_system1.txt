The young man handled money for the first time, paying his own fare on the Paris subway.
When he was 20, his father, Emperor Taisho, sent him abroad _ the first time any member of the immediate imperial family was allowed outside Japan.
His education came as Japan surged into the modern world.
Protocol was maintained at nearly all times and he was prohibited from speaking on any controversial subject.
The Imperial Household Agency, which ran his affairs, said Hirohito considered the postwar period one of the most fulfilling times of his life, in which the nation made a phenomenal recovery from a war that cost 3.1 million Japanese lives.
