Eleven planes from West Germany ferried in 145 tons of supplies plus a field hospital with two medical teams.
``To be on the ground here in Tehran is a very special feeeling for us,'' said Stephen M. Johnson, president of AmeriCares.
Authorities now are faced with the difficult task of getting supplies into the affected areas.
Two countries that also have suffered devastating earthquakes in recent years, the Soviet Union and Mexico, were among those sending assistance to help victims of Thursday's quake.
The shipment that arrived Sunday was the first of its kind to be accepted by Iran since the hostage crisis of 1979-80, when 52 Americans were held at the U.S. Embassy in Tehran.
