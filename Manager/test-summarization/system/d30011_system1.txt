On Sept. 2, Malaysias Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad fired Anwar, calling him morally unfit for office.
Mahathir and Anwar had differed over economic policy and Anwar says Mahathir feared him as an alternative leader.
This week, Mahathir declined to name a replacement for Anwar, who had been Malaysias deputy prime minister since 1993.
After first suggesting that the former deputy prime minister had beaten himself on the face, neck and arms during 10 days in police custody, Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad called for an investigation of the wounds Anwar displayed in court Tuesday and Wednesday.
Anwar said Mahathir viewed him as a threat.
