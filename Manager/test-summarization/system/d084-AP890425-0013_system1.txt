``My first thought was that I'd better get to a lifeboat station, because if the fire got to the magazine things would be bad,'' he said.
The sailors found smoke everywhere as they climbed through hatches and up ladders outside the turret toward the sea deck, Truitt recalled.
When the men discovered that no one was answering the interior turret communications link, Truitt ordered the powder returned to the magazine and the compartment evacuated.
``We were waiting for the gun to fire, and we weren't sure,'' he said.
``I'll be in the turret for the first shot to go off,'' the captain said.
