Look at light created 14 billion years ago and arriving at your eye only this instant.
``No one ever made anything that good,'' James Westphal, California Institute of Technology.
Hubble was an astronomer in the first half of the century who concluded not only that distant galaxies are moving away from Earth but that the farther away they are, the faster they move.
Astronomers think the Big Bang occurred 12 billion to 20 billion years ago and sent the entire universe flying out at incredible speed and heat.
With the Hubble, says scientist Edward Weiler, such pictures are possible any time we want.
