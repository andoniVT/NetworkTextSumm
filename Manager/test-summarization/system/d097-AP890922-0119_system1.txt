As the storm died before dawn, Riley was out on the streets again in a raincoat carrying a flashlight to get a better assessment of the damage.
As the eye of the storm passed, he took reporters onto the porch of the 188-year-old brick City Hall to look at the damage.
During it all, Riley kept in close contact with the National Weather Service.
Later there was a sharp crack as a piece of the tin roof ripped off and water seeped down on the burnished wood tables in the City Council chamber.
On his desk several candles burned as insurance against the inevitable loss of power.
