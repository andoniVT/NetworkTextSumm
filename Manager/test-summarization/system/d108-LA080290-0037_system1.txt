Aug. 2: Iraqi troops invade Kuwait at 2 a.m. local time.
Aug. 1: Unconfirmed reports say Iraq has moved more troops toward the Kuwaiti border.
Diplomats say Iraq has 100,000 troops on Kuwait's border; Kuwait army numbers about 20,000 troops.
July 31: Kuwaiti, Iraqi delegations meet in Jidda, Saudi Arabia, to negotiate oil and border dispute.
Kuwait and the United Arab Emirates pledge to abide by the agreement.
July 26: OPEC ministers in Geneva agree to halt overproduction in hopes of pushing up the price of crude oil.
July 25: Baghdad says that it will not be intimidated by U.S. pressure in its oil dispute with neighboring Kuwait, and Hussein summons the U.S. ambassador for talks.
